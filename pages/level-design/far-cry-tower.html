Far Cry - Tower Traversal Puzzle

Level designed during the Level Design Lobby Bi-Weekly Challenge.
The objective was to develop a level based on the mechanics from the Far Cry series and create a traversal puzzle using the concepts of the towers present on the game series.

Tools:  Unity, Pro Builder (Unity Asset)

Level Brief: 

Design one of the famous Far Cry towers.
The tower should consist of: maximum of 5 enemies, a traversal puzzle, interactions at the top and a maximum duration of 5 minutes.

Design Process: 

References:

After finishing the Far Cry 4 game, I had enough content to build the tower and it's traversal puzzle. But, in order to have a variety of styles in the challenges and puzzles, I watched several gameplays from the previous games and I wrote down some ideas from them.

Basic Mechanics:

When I started brainstorming the ideas for the puzzles, I decided that I should have the following mechanics: crouch, climb a ladder and climb a rope.

For the basic FPS movement and to save some time, I downloaded a FPS Asset from the Unity Asset Store. After that, I customized and developed from scratch the three mechanics that I set as required for the development of the level.

Designing the Tower:

Before starting building the tower, I researched the average size of the towers and I've found out that a tower with 6 floors is the most suitable in order to apply different puzzles and mechanics.

With that in mind, then I started designing the puzzles and applying them to the floors. Using all the information I had previously gathered this task came out to be really fun.

I've tried to use as many puzzles as possible such as: findind the tower entrance, walking on tight spaces and climbing ropes that are high enough to kill the player.

On the last floor, I've added the interaction required in the task: once the player reaches the top, he can hack the signal. The light at the top will then turn to green to show that the tower was successfully hacked.

Feedback:

After some playtesting, I received the following feedbacks and applied them to the level:

  • Pacing: in the first version, there was no enemies on the level. So, after the feedback I've added three enemies at the entrace of the tower to change the pacing;
  • Tower Entrance: once the player reaches the base of the tower, there are "obvious" entrances that are locked. So the player can walk around the tower and see the entrance through a hole in the wall. Even with that, the search was quite easy, so I received the feedback to hide the main entrance with some destructible boxes, another mechanic that can be found on the games.
